Who is Domhnall Gleeson? The Harry Potter star on appearing in Anna Karenina

Larushka Ivan-Zadeh for MetroFriday 15 Feb 2013 6:00 am
Share this article via facebookShare this article via twitterShare this article via messenger
Share this with
Share this article via emailShare this article via flipboardCopy link

Know that face but can’t place the name? ‘It’s like “Tonal” but with a D instead of a T,’ explains Domhnall Gleeson, helpfully. Soon everyone will know how to pronounce it.

While globally recognisable as ginger-haired Bill Weasley from the Harry Potter films, where he co-starred with his dad Brendan Gleeson (aka ‘Mad Eye’ Moody), Gleeson has been quietly building up an ‘actor’s actor’-type portfolio in grown-up Brit indie films such as Never Let Me Go, Shadow Dancer and, most recently, Anna Karenina.

‘I tend to play “tortured” a lot,’ he laughs, ‘whether it’s physically or emotionally.’

In Anna Karenina, based on Tolstoy’s huge Russian novel, which he impressively ‘read quite a few times’, Gleeson won plaudits as Levin, a romantic young idealist that Tolstoy based on himself.

‘He does a lot of thinking,’ muses Gleeson. ‘But when he acts, he acts quite strongly – which I really respect. He’s really kind of cool.’

Next up, the 29-year-old Irishman is taking a break from serious. ‘After working really hard for two years, I thought I’d see if I could try some funny stuff.’

In About Time, he stars as Bill Nighy’s time-travelling son who falls in love with Rachel McAdams. Given it happens to be the new, massively anticipated romcom from Richard Curtis (Four Weddings And A Funeral, Notting Hill, Love Actually), is Gleeson gunning to be the ‘new Hugh’? (Grant, that is). ‘No, no, no,’ he insists. ‘I wouldn’t say that.

‘It’s quite different. I wouldn’t say I’m exactly playing a version of Richard but they say every screenplay is about the screenwriter and Richard is a romantic, who thinks a lot about his family.’

Family also plays a big part in Gleeson’s life. It wasn’t until he was in secondary school that his father Brendan (In Bruges) became famous.

‘He had a really small part in Braveheart but suddenly my friends knew who my father was, which was cool. But there was no confusion about what was important to him – family always came first.’

Gleeson is also developing a career as a director. ‘My dad said, if no one was giving me acting work I’d have to be prepared to create it myself. I would like to keep on working but you never know in this industry.’
